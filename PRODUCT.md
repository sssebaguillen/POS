# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Small and medium business owners in Argentina and LATAM — almacén, kiosko, clothing store, hardware store. They operate the POS themselves as the primary cashier, or delegate to a small team of operators with role-based access. Context: busy counter environment, multiple customers waiting, cash and card transactions. Primary task: ring up a sale fast and accurately.

## Product Purpose

Pulsar POS is a multi-tenant SaaS point-of-sale for SMBs in LATAM. It replaces expensive hardware POS systems with a browser-based tool that costs nothing to run — free plan covers the daily workflow. The product succeeds when a business owner can process a sale, check their day's totals, and manage inventory without any training.

## Brand Personality

Precise, warm, reliable. Like a well-calibrated scale — confident and functional without being cold. The name carries two intentional meanings: the physical act of pressing (tapping the screen) and a neutron star (fast, dense, powerful). Not corporate, not startup-flashy.

## Anti-references

Generic SaaS dashboard look (blue/purple palette, hero metrics with glowing cards). Dated desktop POS UI (grey Windows forms, cluttered toolbars). Anything that looks like it needs a manual to operate.

## Design Principles

1. Speed over completeness — the cashier's clock is always running. Every interaction should complete in one tap or one Enter.
2. Context over documentation — the interface should explain itself through state, not tooltips or help text.
3. Trust the operator — don't make cashiers confirm low-risk actions. Reserve confirmation for genuinely destructive paths.
4. Warm, not cold — the palette and tone should feel like a neighborhood shop, not a bank terminal.
5. Errors are real — stock is finite, prices matter, mistakes cost money. Be honest and direct about warnings.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No explicit WCAG level specified. Touch targets should accommodate tablet POS use (common setup in Argentine small businesses). Both light and dark modes supported.
